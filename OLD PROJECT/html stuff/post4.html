Homework Help

by Fruit Dragon

Tyler desperately needed help.

He’d been staring at the same math problem for seventeen minutes (he’d counted) and still couldn’t make heads or tails of the question. But he couldn’t ask anyone for help; both teachers were preoccupied helping the other study group participants, and those who were not being helped were probably in the same boat as him, or they wouldn’t be in the study group. He checked his watch. Nineteen minutes now.

Flopping over the back of his chair, he groaned. Surely it would be fine if he just skipped that problem, it was the last problem on his homework after all. But his grade in math couldn’t suffer any more than it already did. Sighing, he righted himself and prepared himself to raise his hand. Only to stop midway as the chair beside his slid back, sliding across the tiled floor with an eardrum-shattering scraaaape.

“Hey, Tyler. You need help?” The voice sounded familiar, but it wasn’t until he glanced at the person that he realized who it was. One of his classmates, River. The other kid always raised his hand in class, hence why his voice was so familiar.

“Um, yes please if you wouldn’t mind. Also, why are you in a study group? I thought you were good at school, River,” he said, returning his eyes to the paper in front of him.

River dropped into the chair, swinging his backpack down onto the ground. “I may be good at English and math, but I absolutely suck at history. So Mrs. Brown told me to come and she’d tutor me. What’s the math problem about?” he asked, glancing over Tyler’s shoulder.

Tyler pointed to the last problem on the page, a word problem coupled with a table. “I don’t even know what they want me to look for, let alone what these numbers mean.”

River quickly read over the problem, then smiled. “It’s not so bad. It’s asking you to find the slope of the line, and gave a table full of points that are on the line.”

Tyler shrugged helplessly. “I don’t even know how to find the slope! What do I do?” He flopped onto the table, banging his head, then came back up with an “ow”.

Laughing, River flipped the paper over and began drawing a quick graph. “See, if you plot these points on a graph you get a line. To find the slope you just need to find the number of units a point on the line goes up and then across to the next point. Rise over run.”

Tyler quickly did what River asked, coming up with three. “Is this correct?”

“Yup, exactly!”

Upon hearing that, Tyler grinned and jotted the answer down, boxing it for good measure. “Thank you so much, River! You literally just saved my grade!”

“No problem. If you need anything else, just ask.” River then frowned contemplatively. “You wouldn’t happen to be good at history, would you?”

“That’s the one class I have an A in, why?”

“Maybe you could help me?”

Tyler smiled. “Well, River, what do you have in mind?”
