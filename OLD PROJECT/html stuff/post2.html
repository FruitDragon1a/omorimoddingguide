The Beast

by Fruit Dragon

The Beast huddled
tight,
tighter,
in a tree hollow
wishing it could find a home
and not be left in the cold

The Villagers celebrated
dancing,
laughing,
the Hunter had returned.

Three weeks passed
the hard winter made
harder
by the snow.
Lights glowed in frosted windows.

The Hunter left again
off to chase another
fantastical creature
leaving
the Villagers
once again.

The Beast saw light
glowing,
softly through the snow
frosted windows and firelight.

The Beast walked
into the Village
watched carefully by
the village guards

The Beast gifted the villagers
a wreath made of holly
leaves and berries
they hung it up on the Hunter’s house

The Beast followed them
to a large building
the town hall.
Happy, warm
safe.

There they danced.
The Beast,
the Villagers
all whirling round,
laughing,
singing.

For weeks they celebrated
a new friend,
a new quarry,
extra fish caught
dozens of reasons to celebrate.

The Beast was happier
than ever.
New friends,
a home,
everything the Beast dreamed of.
But it was not to last.

It was yet another party
the third one this week?
when the Hunter returned.
By now,
spring had arrived,
the crop had been planted,
it was warm enough to be outside.
yet another reason to celebrate.

The Beast was dancing
singing,
laughing,
happy with friends.

Then the Hunter saw the Beast.

“Villagers,” the Hunter said.
“Why did you let a monster in?
“Look at it.
“It’s ugly.
“It’s monstrous.
“We must chase it out of the town.
“It is a menace.”

The Villagers were shocked.
The Beast angry.
One thing led to another
and the Beast snapped
at the Hunter.

The Villagers grew mad.
“We fed this thing,” one said.
“We let it stay.”
“We celebrated with it.”
“And it repays us by trying to kill the Hunter.”
“Not the Hunter!”

Grabbing their torches
and pitchforks
the happy Villagers
soon turned angry.

The Beast had no choice but to
run
    run
        run
far away from the village
as far as the Beast could run.
farther than the Villagers could run.
only a little farther
than the Hunter could run.

Sad,
hurt,
confused,
the Beast hid
deep in a cave
in case the Villagers
came looking.
They never came.

Weeks
turned to months
turned to years
still the Beast never left
hoping against hope
at least one Villager would look
at least one.

And what of the Villagers?
As far as
the Beast knows
they are still celebrating
to this very day.

And the Hunter
He got what was coming
a few months after
he disappeared
only to meet the same fate
the Beast did
at a different village.
