Dreams

Part 1

by Fruit Dragon

“Wake up.”

Amanda groggily lifted her head, annoyed. “What, Mom? It’s like, midnight. Leave me alone.”

“Wake up, Amanda. I’m not your mother. You must come with me.”

At that, Amanda’s head cleared and her eyes shot open. “Who are you? How’d you get in my house?” She couldn’t see anyone, however; the darkness was too all-encompassing. She fumbled for her nightstand lamp.

Homework Help

by Fruit Dragon

Tyler desperately needed help.

He’d been staring at the same math problem for seventeen minutes (he’d counted) and still couldn’t make heads or tails of the question. But he couldn’t ask anyone for help; both teachers were preoccupied helping the other study group participants, and those who were not being helped were probably in the same boat as him, or they wouldn’t be in the study group. He checked his watch. Nineteen minutes now.

Flopping over the back of his chair, he groaned. Surely it would be fine if he just skipped that problem, it was the last problem on his homework after all. But his grade in math couldn’t suffer any more than it already did.

The Beast

by Fruit Dragon

The Beast huddled
tight,
tighter,
in a tree hollow
wishing it could find a home
and not be left in the cold

The Villagers celebrated
dancing,
laughing,
the Hunter had returned.

Three weeks passed
the hard winter made
harder
